Observations

We see from our results that when we have a low ks value, our cloth has more waves as it is more malleable, since a lower spring constant would mean that there is less force tugging on each point mass. Conversely, we see that a cloth with a high spring constant has less waves, because there is more tension between the point masses. Similarly, with spring constants, the density of the cloth also affects the amount of ripples the cloth has. Instead, when there is a higher density, there are more ripples because we have more points per area, similarly to having a finer level of detail which would allow for more, smaller ripples, while low densities create stiffer clothes. Damping on the other hand produces a different effect. The two images above are the states of the cloth mesh after 7 seconds from the beginning resting state. Through these images, we can see that higher damping causes the cloth to move more slowly, since damping factors such as friction and heat loss tend to negate movement and point displacement.
